Model Results Visualizations

This matrix shows the actual vs. predicted classifications of fetal health by the model, where each cell color intensity indicates the count of predictions.

The model performs well in predicting Normal cases but shows some errors, particularly in distinguishing between Suspect and Pathological cases.
This bar chart ranks features based on their importance in influencing the model's predictions.

Features like abnormal short-term variability and mean value of short-term variability appear to be most influential.
This graph shows the training and cross-validation scores as a function of the number of training examples used.

Both curves converge, indicating that adding more training examples might not significantly improve the model's performance. The model is stable, with a good generalization as seen from the close performance scores between training and validation.
This Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curve shows the true positive rate against the false positive rate for different decision thresholds.

The abrupt rise and high area under the curve (AUC) close to 1 suggest that the model has excellent discriminative ability to separate the classes, particularly between Normal and more severe conditions.
