EDA Visualizations

The bar chart and pie chart show the count of cases classified as Normal, Suspect and Pathological in the Fetal Health dataset.

The majority of the cases are classified as Normal (1655 cases), Suspect cases are significantly fewer (295), and Pathological cases are the least (176).

The distrubtion also highlights the class imbalance present in the dataset.
This density plot overlays the distribution of baseline fetal heart rates for each fetal health category.

The Normal category colored in green has a broader and slightly shifted distribution towards higher heart rates, suggesting a variability that remains within healthy limits.

Both Suspect (blue) and Pathological (purple) categories show narrower distributions, which might indicate specific heart rate patterns associated with these conditions.
This heatmap shows the correlation coefficients between different features in the dataset.

High correlations (green) between certain features like accelerations and fetal movement could indicate strong relationships that are significant in predicting fetal health.

Features with low or negative correlations might be less informative individually but could still contribute to predictive models when combined.
